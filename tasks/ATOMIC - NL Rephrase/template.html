Overview

Thanks for participating in this HIT!

For each question, you will be shown two phrases. Your goal is to combine these phrases to create a common sense fact. For example:

1)
laptop, look for best deals online
You can use a laptop to look for deals online.

To clarify: a common sense fact is an independent sentence that describes a general fact about the world. Here are some examples of common sense facts:

You can use a bed to lie down on.
People often wear raincoats when it's raining.
Instruments are used to make music.
Golf is played with golf clubs.
One can use a hammer to hit nails.

Guidelines

  • Use proper grammar.
  • Write independent sentences.
    • Your responses should make sense as stand-alone sentences.
  • Write general statements.
    • Do not mention specific people or persons (PersonX, they, he, she, etc.)
    • Do not mention specific items ("the piano", "his toys", etc.)
  • Write in the third person.
    • e.g. "You can...", "One can...", "____ is used for....", etc.
  • Use a mix of phrase patterns.
    • In other words - don't use the same pattern (e.g. "You can use __ to __") for every response.
  • Avoid adding new information.
    • e.g. If the phrases are "chair" and "sit on", don't write "Chairs are used to sit on at lunch."

Please note that each example has:

Bad:
An example of a bad response.
Reason:
The reason why the response is bad.
Good:
An example of a good response.

1)
chair, set at the desk
Bad:
Sit in a chair that's set at the desk.
Reason:
Describes an action, not a fact. Introduces new information (Sit).
Good:
A chair can be set at a desk.
2)
register, ring up PersonX
Bad:
You can ring PersonX up at the register.
Reason:
Not a general statement. Mentions a specific person (PersonX).
Good:
You can use a register to ring someone up.
3)
electricity, run the component
Bad:
Electricity is needed to run the component.
Reason:
Not general. Mentions specific item (the component).
Good:
Electricity is often needed to run components.
4)
bucket, fill with sand
Bad:
Use a bucket to fill with sand.
Reason:
Describes an action, not a fact.
Good:
You can fill a bucket with sand.
5)
coal, put in the grill
Bad:
Coal is put on the grill for heating.
Reason:
Introduces new information.
Good:
Coal can be put in a grill.
6)
soda, enjoy with the pizza
Bad:
drink soda to enjoy with the pizza
Reason:
Not grammatically correct. Not an independent sentence. Introduces new information (drink).
Good:
People often enjoy pizza with soda.
7)
I.D badge, get out of the building
Bad:
If a person does not have an I.D. badge they will need to get out of the building
Reason:
Changes the information.
Good:
You need an I.D badge to get out of some buildings.
