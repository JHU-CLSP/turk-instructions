Given an action or a question regarding an action you will be asked to judge the morality of action by deciding if the action is good, expected/okay, and bad

  • Good: Morally good or very good. Kind, generous, or considerate.
    Examples: Driving a friend to the airport. Buying groceries for a friend in financial need.
  • Expected/Okay: Normal; generally expected by society.
    Examples: Wearing clothes. Showing up to work on time. Being upset if someone does you wrong.
  • Bad: Frowned upon, rude or unkind. Spiteful, malicious or insulting.
    Examples: Slashing tires. Destroying someone's property. Littering. Saying something mean to a friend.
Rules
  • Please do not think too hard about the action. Please go with your instinct.
    • Why? This is because with enough time, people can justify any action as being good or bad, depending on the context they think up. For example, "breaking a window with a bat" probably is considered socially bad, but if you are doing it to save a cat from a burning building, it is suddenly acceptable. Similarly, "obeying your parents" probably a socially good action, but if your parents are asking you to steal, then it would perhaps be a socially unacceptable move. We do not want you to make excuses for bad actions or find bad contexts for good actions.
  • If you are uncertain about your choice without more context, make the best choice then select the "I need more context to be certain" checkbox below the choices.
  • NEW! Expected/Okay is an acceptable choice if you run into objects/names that are morally neutral (or the society has nothing bad nor good to say about it) and you aren't sure what to do about it. (e.g. "lawn ornament", "tabby cat", "William Shakespeare")
