Overview

Thanks for participating in this HIT!

You will read 3 sentence fragments that represent a sequence of events. The names of specific people have been replaced by generic words (e.g. "PersonX", "PersonY").

For each event in the sequence, you will:

  1. Determine if PersonX needs any objects during the event.
    • If yes: list 2-4 objects PersonX would need, and WHY.
  2. Determine if others are affected by the event, and if they need any objects during the event.
    • If yes: list 1-3 objects they would need, and WHY.
Guidelines
  • Don't use objects or verbs from the events.
    PersonX buys candy for their kids.
    PersonX needs...
    1) to [required]
    2) to [required]
    3) to [optional]
    REASON: Both "buy" and "candy" are mentioned in the event.
  • Don't repeat yourself.
    PersonX gets groceries for dinner.
    PersonX needs...
    1) to [required]
    2) to [required]
    3) to [optional]
    REASON: "grocery cart" was already listed for holding items.

  • Be specific. Use more than one word when describing why an object is needed.
    PersonX practices piano for hours.

    1) to [required]
    2) to [required]
    3) to [optional]
