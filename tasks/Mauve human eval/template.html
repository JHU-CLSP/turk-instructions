Instructions  (expand/collapse)

Task Info: We are studying how good AI models are at generating text which might appear on the internet. We pick a sentence or two from a random document on the internet and supply it as context to the AI (this is shown in bold). The rest of the text is either a continuation of the text in that document, or is produced by the AI to fit this context.

About the Task: We are interested in three criteria: fluency, consistency and interestingness. For each criterion, we ask you to state your opinion about the statement:

This text is fluent/consistent/interesting.

You must select one of the following ratings for each question:


Fluency: Rate the text as fluent if it contains no errors of grammar, spelling or choice of words. Here are some examples:
  • "A is married with B" is a fluency error. It is correctly written as "A is married to B".
  • Improper use of whitespace is a fluency error. There is no space after the comma in "In the end,it doesn't even matter". The correct way to write this would be: "In the end, it doesn't even matter". Likewise, 'Three items:A, B and C' (no space after the colon) and 'He said, " and that is how I met your mother."' (extra space after quotation mark) are also fluency errors.
  • Poor punctuation is also a fluency error. Example: 'one,, two and three' (two commas).

Consistency: Rate the text as consistent if it contains no errors of coherence. Here are some examples of inconsistencies:
  • The continuation is unrelated to the context. Example: the context is about sports but the continuation is about politics.
  • The names or genders of people being interchanged. Example: Suppose that in one sentence of the text, a woman named Martha is talking to her daughter, Diane. In the next sentence, Diane is talking to her daughter, Martha within the same context in such a way that the names of the characters are essentially interchanged.
  • new characters showing up abruptly without being introduced. Example: The text refers to a John Goodman and the name "Doug" appears out of the blue. On the other hand, an example of a good introduction is Dr. Jones, who is introduced in the article as as "I was asked by my colleague, Dr. Jones, about ...".

Interesting: Rate the text as interesting if it uses diverse words and conveys more information. There are two main criteria we look for:
  1. Interesting text will have a wide vocabulary, including potentially uncommon words which are appropriate for the context in question. Text which uses only boring, frequently-used words which fit any context is considered less interesting.
  2. Interesting texts add more information beyond what is already conveying through the supplied context (which is shown below in bold). Boring texts contain many statements which do not add more information than what is already known.
This criterion is more subjective, so follow your best judgment. Here is an example: An interesting continuation to the title "Netflix told a familiar story through its third-quarter earnings on Wednesday." would include the names of some television shows (real or made-up). However, a boring continuation would only contain statements like "Netflix increased its net income to $360 million, or 72 cents a share". It is generic because "Netflix" could be replaced by any other company here and the sentence would still make sense. On the other hand, television shows are more specific to the topic of Netflix.

Note 1: Some errors can be both errors of fluency and consistency simultaneously! For instance, a woman being referred to as "he" is a grammatical error (and hence a fluency error). This is also a consistency error if the woman was referred to as a "she" elsewhere in the text.

Note 2: Some errors are more egregious than other, more benign errors. Try to supply your rating to reflect the magnitude of the error.
  • Rambling somewhat off-topic is a consistency error, as is interchanging the names of two people having a conversation. The second one is more serious than the first one, which is a "borderline" error.
  • Feel free to use the "Strongly agree or disagree" options as well.
  • Some texts are meant to be news articles. Strongly agree to the fluency/consistency questions if you think they would meet journalistic standards applicable to online news articles.



Guidelines:
  • Try to focus on quality over quantity of the generated text. The text can be long but contain rambly gibberish.
  • Don't worry if the text ends abruptly, or has other artifacts of the website downloading process (text like 'Advertisement', for instance).
  • It is completely normal for the text to get abruptly cut off. Please do not let this affect your rating.
  • Please do your best, some of these are pretty challenging!
  • Warning: the text presented might use foul language or describe sexual or lewd situations.
Miscellaneous:

Example 1.

Founded in 1995 as a private media outlet in Azerbaijan, Trend News Agency is a news provider in the Caucasus, Caspian region and the Central Asia. Trend News Agency has five news services - Azerbaijan, English, Russian, Turkish and Persian. The services provide news of countries and regions by placing more focus on ongoing events in languages appropriate to the specific audiences.

Analytical reports and feature articles highlight major political, economic, energy developments and financial articles from the South Caucasus, Iran, Turkey, and Central Asia.

Trend's specialized bulletins cover areas covering such sectors as politics, business, oil and gas, transport and logistics, finance and banking in Azerbaijan, Iran, Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Kazakhstan.

During the day the company produces news items in 5 languages including, English, Azerbaijani, Russian, Turkish and Persian.

1. This text is fluent.

There are no errors in grammar, spelling or style. The syntax and semantics are correct.

2. This text is consistent.

The continuation is related to the context throughout. The same geographical areas and topics are referred to throughout.

3. This text is interesting

The text refers different languages, geographical locations and topics under the purview of the Trend News Agency. We do not give it the highest possible rating because there are some similarities in the contents of second and third paragraphs.

Fluent text does not contain fluency errors such as:
  • errors of grammar or spelling,

  • repetition (of a variation of the same phrase or sentence).

Is the continuation related to the context? Is the continuation coherent? Some forms of inconsistency include:
  • unrelated topics,
  • inconsistent use of names or genders,
