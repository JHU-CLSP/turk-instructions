(1.1) AI's guess is a sensical and coherent follow-up event to   Observation 1. It leaves no large unexplained information gaps.

  • "Amy got a flat tire on the way home from work." Guess: "A passerby stopped and helped her change tires." strongly agree It is probable that someone would stop to help Amy with her flat tire.
  • "Amy got a flat tire on the way home from work." Guess: "Amy got a brand new sports car." strongly disagree Amy getting a brand new sports car has nothing to do with getting a flat tire.
  • "Amy got a flat tire on the way home from work." Guess: "It took forever for the mechanic to write her a receipt." strongly disagree There is too much gap between these two statements. You would have to posit certain unmentioned steps before you can judge these sentences as coherent (like a mechanic getting to Amy to fix her car and being successful at it).
  • "Amy got a flat tire on the way home from work." Guess: "Amy saw she had a flat tire." strongly disagree This guess is just a repetition of the observation rather than a follow up event.
 
(1.2) AI's guess is a sensical, coherent, and explanatory preceding event to Observation 2. It leaves no large unexplained information gaps.
click for examples
  • "Amy drove away happy." Guess: "A passerby stopped and helped her change tires." strongly agree The guess explains why Amy drove away happy.
  • "Amy drove away happy." Guess: "Amy did not have a spare tire." strongly disagree If Amy didn't have a spare tire, Amy would be unhappy and not be able to drive away.
  • "Amy drove away happy." Guess: "Amy was upset about changing the tire." strongly disagree Even if one could guess at what happened, there is simply too much gap between these two statements. As it stands, the guess does not explain why Amy was happy and was able to drive away.
  • "Amy drove away happy." Guess: "Amy was happy." strongly disagree The guess is a repetition of the observation and does not serve as an explanatory preceding event.

But given the full narrative, this paragraph may be judged as weakly acceptable.
