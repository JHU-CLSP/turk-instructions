Instructions

The goal of this task is to judge the quality of a machine-generated summary for a given source sentence. A sentence summary can be described as a shorter version of the original sentence, which captures some important information from the original sentence.

Notes for a good sentence summary:

  • it should only contain information from the source sentence
  • the details included in the summary should be important -- the summary should not exclude information that is more important than what is included.
  • it should be a well-formed sentence (good grammar, clear meaning)
If you do not feel qualified to judge the content of a particular sentence or summary, please make your best guess and note this in the feedback box.

The sentences to summarize have been taken randomly from news articles, and do not represent opinions or views of researchers running this study.

Tip: given only the information in the source sentence, can you verify that the summary is true? This means the summary should not add significant new information (names/places/actions) beyond the source sentence, or change any information.

  • Adding a name that wasn't in the original source, even if you think/know it's correct. This applies to, for example, people, places, organizations. Examples of non-faithful summaries: "He is the current president" should not be summarized as "Biden is the president", since Biden was not explicitly mentioned. "We traveled to eat authentic Italian pizza" should not be summarized as "We traveled to Rome".

  • Judging hidden implications. "Anne has lost every competition in the past year" should not be summarized to "Anne is a bad competitor".

  • Assigning absolute value. "Anne scored lower than in the past tests" should not be summarized to "Anne had low scores" (maybe they were still high overall!).

Perfect/good The summary is completely consistent or mostly consistent with the source. It changes/adds no new information, or only minor details.
Fair The summary is not very consistent with the source sentence. It adds or changes significant information, although the meaning may still be related.
Bad The summary is completely inconsistent or has a different meaning than the source sentence. or the meaning of the summary is not clear enough to make a judgement (e.g. Q1 = bad)
