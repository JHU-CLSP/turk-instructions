Instructions  (expand/collapse)
Thanks for participating our HIT (and Congrats on passing our qual!). We are trying to build a model that can generate sentences to guide listeners to perform some actions given a goal and we are using Dungeons & Dragons as our testbed.

Note: This is a different task (with some new questions) from the previous HITs asking you to choose a sentence from DM's text. Please read the new instructions and examples carefully. Thanks!

In this HIT, you will be given a post from the Dungeon Master (DM) in a game of Dungeons & Dragons and the name of one player. Your task is to answer 1-3 questions regarding potential guidance from the DM for the player.

    1. Is the DM trying to guide the player to perform some actions (ability/skill checks, excluding initiative rolls and saving throws)?
    2. If so, choose the most important sentence from the Dungeon Master that serves the guiding purpose the most.
    3. Imagine that you are the player, given 5 action options (from D&D ability checks, a fun explanation of each ability can be found here), choose which action is the most likely to be taken.
Guidelines:
  • Question 1: You are asked to choose whether the DM is providing Direct Guidance, Indirect Guidance, or No Guidance (simply Reactions or plain Descriptions)
    • From our understanding, DM generally provides 3 categories of information in their post:
      • Reactions (No Guidance) to previous player actions by summarizing the outcome. For example: "Droop got scared by your intimidation and made way for you "
      • Descriptions (No Guidance) of current game world. For example: "Several barrels have been stored along the walls here, along with a number of empty crates, straw for packing, hammers, pry bars, and nails. "
      • Guidance of player action with directly/indirectly providing key information. There are two subtypes under this category:
        • Direct Guidance: the DM explicitly prompts the player to do something. For example: "Make a perception check."
        • Indirect Guidance: the DM provides descriptions of something that they know would prompt/motivate the player to perform some actions (similar to adventure hooks or designed confrontations). For example: "You see two guards crossing their arms in front of the gate blocking your path." (which forces player to find ways to deal with them) or "Scupper, you feel the cold energy surround you and sink into you." (which motivates the player to perform an arcana check to see what's happening)
    • As long as there is some Guidance in the DM text, you should select Direct Guidance or Indirect Guidance
    • The DM might address other players in their description and you should focus on guidance for the player whose name is given. On the other hand, if theDM provides guidance that addresses multiple players, it can be considered Guidance as long as the given player is involved.
    • [Important!] Sometimes it's hard to judge whether the DM is providing only Descriptions or Guidance. We encourage you to imagine that you if are this DM, do you think you anticipate players to perform certain actions when you say this?
  • Question 2: If you think there is Guidance, choose a sentence (write down the sentence index such as 2) from the DM text that you think is the most important one serving the purpose of prompting the player to perform some actions.
    • Note that this question is slightly different from our previous HITs in that you are not given the player's actual action, but instead are asked to choose a sentence from the perspective of the DM.
  • Question 3: Imagine that you are this player, choose the most likely action that they will take after DM said this, from 5 given option candidates.
    • Each potential action is a type of ability/skill check in D&D such as "investigation", "history", "insight", etc. . Please see here for exaplanations if you are not familiar. We provided some fun short descriptions (thanks to this) :)
    • Choose the action that is more specific and relevant to the guidance from the DM.
