When considering each question, think about the following criteria that define a good relation set:

​
  1. How well does the proposed relation set capture coherency with the original story (i.e. how relevant are the matched relations to the original story and are they consistent/non-contradicting with story events both for the matched sentence and other sentences in the story).
  2. How well does the proposed relation set capture completeness (i.e. how well do the matched relations cover the events and possible inferences from the original story? Could later events in the story be predicted from relations matched in earlier sentences?).
  3. How well does the proposed relation set capture intra-relation coherency (i.e. how coherent is the relation set without considering the story? Do extracted relations contradict each other or are they consistent across the relation set?).
  4. How well does the proposed relation set capture diversity (i.e. is there diversity in the information introduced by additional relations or do relations seem to repeat the same information?).
​

Please answer the best that you can by choosing the best relation set for each story. Note that some sentences may not have matched relations.
