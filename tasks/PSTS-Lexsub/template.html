Paraphrases are words or phrases with approximately similar meaning, like bug and glitch.

Some words like bug can take on different meanings, depending on how they are used. For example, bug can refer to a computer problem, a creepy crawly pest, or a microorganism that makes people sick. In each case, bug has a different set of paraphrases.

This task asks you to determine whether a paraphrase is appropriate for a target word in a specific context. We will show you a sentence containing a target word, and ask you whether one of its paraphrases is applicable in that specific sentence. The possible answers are:

  • YES The target word has roughly the same meaning as the paraphrase in this particular sentence.
  • NO The target word has a different meaning from the paraphrase in this sentence (but the target word can mean the same thing as the paraphrase in other contexts).
  • UNCLEAR There is not enough information to tell.
  • NEVER Not a valid paraphrase. The target word and the proposed paraphrase never have similar meaning. 

 

Here are some examples:

  • Test Sentence We're looking into a bug that was planted in the office ceiling.
    Paraphrase microphone

     

    Answer: YES. Here bug refers to a hidden listening device, or microphone.  
  • Test Sentence There 's a nasty bug going around.
    Paraphrase microbe

     

    Answer: YES. Here bug and microbe both refer to some type of microorganism that makes people sick.  
  • Test Sentence The bug crawled up my arm.
    Paraphrase microphone

     

    Answer: NO. In the test sentence, bug refers to an organism, not a device.  
  • Test Sentence The bug crawled up my arm.
    Paraphrase photography

     

    Answer: NEVER. Photography is never a good paraphrase for bug.  

 

These sentences have been pre-processed by converting all letters to lowercase, separating punctuation, and splitting conjunctions. Please ignore this in your work and do not allow it to affect your judgement of meaning similarity.

Also, for some target words, replacing the target with the paraphrase will lead to incorrect grammar. Please ignore grammaticality and spelling in your work, and concentrate only on whether the meaning of the paraphrase is roughly equivalent to the target in the given sentence.

These HITs will be quality controlled. If you are unsure whether or not you are doing them well, we recommend you do a small number and wait for feedback before continuing.
